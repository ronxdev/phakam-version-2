The Letter

“If we can’t all rise, we can’t rise at all.” Growing up, I heard my grandfather speak a lot about ubuntu. Ubuntu this, ubuntu that; hell, there were even t-shirts being printed at a point that read “do you ubuntu?” Ubuntu is, in essence, humanity in its purest form. More than a term it’s a way of life – I am who I am because you are who you are. I excel because you excel, I fail because you fail. My life, my success, my being is inherently tied to everyone else’s around me. With that in mind, how do we as a nation expect to succeed when less than 6% of women of colour (WOC) are finishing high school and making it to tertiary education? How do we wish to achieve “first world” status when the majority of our population have third world opportunities? I was blessed enough to truly grow up knowing that the world was at my fingertips: went to the best schools, I’ve travelled the world, I’ve experienced people and cultures so far removed from my own it was hard to imagine that we lived on the same planet But what about the girls who aren’t like me? The ones who don’t have a famous grandfather or A-class education to prop them up? I hope to “bridge the gap” between women/girls of color and their white contemporaries. I don’t want it to be a lack of opportunity that kept anyone from achieving greatness because, let’s face it – hard work can only get you so far without the right tools and resources. Together we can. At phakamAfrika I wish to use a holistic approach starting by connecting with the elders, who play an integral role in our society, empowering them with the tools to further empower through their support of progress. By using a three tiered incentive-based model: starting with a holiday day camp, then moving to a curriculum based after school program and ending with New Horizons an international au pair recruitment agency, I hope to incentivize staying in school as well as provide the members of the community with the tools and resources to help them rise. Although my focus is on community outreach, I do not plan on phakamAfrika being an NPO, instead I would like to be seen as a social entreprenuer - making a profit so that I can put something back into the economy and give someone else a chance in the endless fight for funding. Please will you join me on my mission?

About Us

Objective
To empower women/girls of color (WOC) through social development and education.
Goals
• To help "bridge the gap" that has occurred owing to lack of education and opportunities received
• To use a "holistic" approach that works with the dynamics of families of color (starting with elders and working down)
• To dismantle the idea that POC are "victims" - a notion that is instilled when organizations, churches etc come to the communities on "missions"
• To provide the community with resources, time, and support to enable them to uplift themselves and in turn, the country
Solution
Three-tiered program consisting of a holiday day camp, curriculum based after-school program (focusing on four major sectors: computer literacy, mental health, sexual education, and job hunting), and New Horizons - an international au pair recruitment agency.
